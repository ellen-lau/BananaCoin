Credit score is used by lenders to determine if you are a good candidate for a loan.

Higher scores are better, and give you better rates and acccess to more finanical products. You can check your score from your bank. which helps to get approval for future big loans, like a mortgage or an auto loan.

Potential Mistakes



Paying Only the Minimum Payment

Many people make the assumption that paying only the minimum payment will let them get off scot-free, but this is not the case. You will still accrue debt interest on the rest of the unpaid bill.

Paying the minimum payment is just to keep your account in good standing Good Standing

A credit card account is considered to be in good standing if you at the very least make the minimum payments month after month before the deadline.

Losing this status can cause extra interest payments, lose of rewards, and/or a hit to your credit score. with the card issuer.


















Reviewing Your Billing Statement

Before you pay your credit card balance, make sure to check that the transactions recorded on your billing statement match up with the purchases you made.

Sometimes vendors may accidentally double charge you, or canceled transactions may still charge your account; habitually reviewing your billing statement helps you prevent these unauthorized charges.
